How We Handle Your Data

This policy explains what we collect, why we collect it, and the controls available to you. It applies to the Arbitrum website, related content, and the connected dApp experience.

Last updated: April 9, 2026

1. Information We Collect

We may collect information you provide directly, information collected automatically, and information received from service providers or authentication partners.

  • Contact details such as name, email address, and communication preferences.
  • Technical data such as device type, browser, IP address, and session activity.
  • Usage data including pages viewed, clicks, referral sources, and engagement patterns.
  • Account and transaction-related data when you use our dApp or request services.

2. How We Use Information

We use information to operate the website, provide services, respond to requests, improve performance, and maintain security. We may also use information to communicate updates, deliver support, and comply with legal or regulatory obligations.

3. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies, pixels, and similar tools to keep the site functioning, remember preferences, measure performance, and understand how visitors interact with the platform. You can control cookies through your browser settings, but some parts of the site may not function correctly if you disable them.

4. Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell personal information. We may share data with trusted service providers that help us host, secure, support, or analyze the site and services. We may also disclose information if required by law, to protect our rights, to prevent abuse, or in connection with a corporate transaction.

5. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to reduce risk. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as needed to provide services, comply with obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, or support legitimate business purposes. Retention periods may vary based on the type of data and the reason it was collected.

7. Your Choices

  • You may request access, correction, or deletion of certain information where applicable.
  • You may opt out of non-essential communications using the unsubscribe mechanism or by contacting us directly.
  • You can manage cookies through your browser or device settings.

8. Third-Party Services

Our site may link to or integrate with third-party services, including analytics, hosting, wallet, authentication, or payment partners. Their privacy practices are governed by their own policies, not ours.

9. International Users

If you access the site from outside our operating region, your information may be processed in jurisdictions that may have different data protection rules than your own.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the effective date above and post the updated version here.

11. Contact

If you have questions or requests about this policy, contact us through the channels provided on the website or in your account dashboard.
